My Experience in PC Building

I have been building computers for close to 8 years. My first computer build was back in 2011, which was based around an AMD FX-6300 CPU. I loved the experience and have continued on to build for friends and, on occasion, a special request from a paying customer. I have always charged very little for my services, mainly for the fact that I enjoy doing it.

Software

I run Windows 10 Pro, given that the new Ryzen Architecture only supports Windows 10, which I found out when I tried to use Windows 7 Pro. Other programs I use frequently are:

Steam - used for games and benchmarking/stability tests

Microsoft Office 2016 - used for school assignments and productivity

Mozilla Firefox - my main web browser for daily use

Google Chrome - specifically used for online work, school work, and MyLab assignments

Why I chose these parts

Best parts for the Price

I was strolling down at the local swap meet when I was drawn toward a specific package in a pile of random items. It turned out to be a 16GB kit of DDR4 RAM. I asked for the price, expecting it to be very expensive, only to find out it was $2.00, yes, I bought 16GB of DDR4 RAM for $2.00. I was excited at this point, because RAM prices was the barrier that kept me from building a new PC that I sorely needed. My entire build cost me just over $260, because I reused parts from old builds and my extra parts bin. Finding the RAM kit at the swap meet was a major cost savings.
